Safety & Security

If you encounter a fake profile or scammer:

1. Don't respond to suspicious messages
2. Click "Report" on their profile (bottom of the page)
3. Select the reason (fake profile, scammer, spam, etc.)
4. Provide details - Include specific examples of suspicious behavior
5. Block the user to prevent further contact

Our moderators review all reports. Common scam signs include: asking for money, sharing suspicious links, pushing to communicate off-site quickly, or using stolen photos.

General Safety Tips:

• Protect your personal info - Don't share your full name, address, phone number, or financial information early on
• Stay on InterPals - Keep conversations on our platform where we can help if issues arise
• Video chat first - Before meeting or sharing personal details, verify the person via video chat
• Trust your instincts - If something feels wrong, it probably is
• Report suspicious behavior - Help keep our community safe

Remember: Genuine friends will respect your boundaries and won't pressure you.

NEVER send money to someone you've only met online.

If someone asks for money:
1. Stop communicating with them immediately
2. Report their profile using the report button
3. Block the user to prevent further contact
4. Don't feel guilty - Real friends don't ask for money online

Common scam stories include: medical emergencies, travel problems, business opportunities, or romantic sob stories. These are ALL scams. InterPals strictly prohibits financial requests between members.

Red flags to watch for:

• Too good to be true - Extremely attractive photos, perfect life story
• Fast romance - Declares love quickly without meeting
• Avoids video chat - Always has excuses not to video call
• Asks for money - Any financial request is a scam
• Pushes off-site - Wants to move to email/WhatsApp immediately
• Poor language - Inconsistent grammar, copy-pasted messages
• Suspicious links - Sends links to unknown websites

Trust your instincts and report suspicious profiles immediately.

Language Exchange

Language exchange on InterPals is simple:

1. Set your languages in your profile - Languages you speak and languages you're learning
2. Search for partners - Find native speakers of your target language who want to learn your language
3. Connect and practice - Exchange messages, do video calls, and help each other learn
4. Be patient and helpful - Good language exchange is mutual: spend equal time on both languages

Tips: Look for partners at similar skill levels, set regular practice times, and use a mix of text and voice practice.

To find native speakers:

1. Go to Search
2. Under "Languages Spoken", select your target language
3. Set the minimum level to "Native" or "Proficient"
4. Optionally, filter by country where the language is spoken
5. Look for people who want to learn YOUR native language for best matches

Pro tip: Read profiles carefully - the best language partners are those with similar interests and learning goals.

Languages I speak: Your native language(s) and any languages you're already proficient or conversational in. Other members might contact you for help with these languages.

Languages I'm learning: Languages you want to practice and improve. This helps others know what languages you need help with.

Set these correctly on your profile page to find the best language exchange partners. You can set your skill level for each language (beginner, intermediate, advanced, proficient, native).
